Empowered by Hardships: A Journey Toward Success



Embracing Growth

     If I had known my life would one day be a story to share, I would have started writing a diary long ago.1 A boy named Jerome was born into a not-so-privileged family.2 He was the first-born child of two hardworking parents—his father, a tricycle driver, and his mother, a server at a fast-food restaurant.3 In his infant and toddler years, he lived with his parents in a small apartment in Rizal.4 However, when he turned four, his mother had to work overseas to support their family.5 From that moment on, his aunt and grandparents took care of him.6 Jerome spent the rest of his formative years with his grandparents and enjoyed helping out at their food stall.7 His grandfather would often take him to their province in Quezon without his parents’ permission, but Jerome loved every moment of it.8He cherished the feeling of sand between his toes, the simple life in the barrio, and, most of all, the beautiful beach where he learned to swim.9 Jerome’s preschool years were filled with achievements—he ranked Top 1 in nursery, excelled in most subjects, and was again Top 1 in kindergarten.10 Because of his strong performance, he was accelerated to Grade 1 in a private school.11 However, he was later transferred to a public school, where he experienced a cultural shift that took time to adjust to.12 From Grades 1 to 4, he didn’t make many friends and barely remembers his experiences with them.13 After four years of working abroad, Jerome’s mother returned to the Philippines when he was around seven years old.14 A year later, at the age of eight, he became an older brother, filling his heart with love and happiness.15Three years later, he welcomed another sibling, a baby sister.16 In Grade 5, Jerome stepped out of his comfort zone and participated in a Sabayang Pagbigkas competition.17 Although his team placed 6th, he enjoyed the experience.18 Determined, he tried again in Grade 6, but this time he joined a BigSayWit competition.19 His team won Champion in Rodriguez and placed 4th in Rizal, marking one of the highlights of his elementary school years. 20



Finding Confidence

     As Jerome entered his teenage years, his parents opened a business that slowly changed their lives for the better.1 He also gained another sibling—a younger brother, who is now the youngest in the family.2 Jerome attended his dream school in his hometown, a Catholic institution with beautiful gardens, impressive buildings, and a stunning mountain view.3 The school was wonderful, but Jerome faced challenges.4 The students there were exceptionally talented, and Jerome struggled to keep up.5 In Grade 7, his grades were barely satisfactory, but over time, he continued to improve.6 Despite his progress, his experiences at the school led to a decline in his self-esteem, which caused a fear of stepping outside his comfort zone.7 Jerome graduated from Grade 10 with honors but decided to transfer schools.8 He asked his parents if he could study at the University of the East in Manila as an ICT track student.9 Thankfully, his parents agreed.10 At his new school, Jerome flourished.11 With new classmates and a fresh environment, he was able to do all the things he had wanted to at his former school.12 He became a student leader, joined various organizations, aimed for excellence, and was even elected as a student council officer.13 Jerome graduated Senior High School with high honors through hard work.14 He was deeply grateful for the support he received from his family and friends throughout his academic journey.15 Along the way, Jerome also learned a great deal about his family’s business.16 By the age of 14, he was capable of running the entire business.17 He often accompanied his mother to business meetings, and over the years, Jerome became a business-minded individual whom his parents could always trust.18 Due to his experiences, Jerome became a role model for his younger siblings, and he promised to continue doing his best to make his parents proud.19 With his past shaping him, Jerome entered college ready to embrace new challenges and opportunities on his journey forward.20



Pursuing Dreams

      Jerome, considering his future, saw his dream of becoming a game developer fade due to the uncertain prospects of the profession in this country.1 He opted for the BSIT program at the University of the East.2 In his first semester, Jerome found the material easy to grasp, as it felt like a repetition of what he had learned in Senior High School.3 He enjoyed his college experience, with a more flexible schedule that allowed him to explore new places and make new friends.4 His hard work paid off when he earned a college scholarship.5 During his second semester, Jerome met someone special, and they supported each other in their studies while enjoying cute dates together.6 However, Jerome caught chickenpox halfway through the semester, which caused him to fall behind.7 Despite the setback, he remained determined to keep his grades up.8 Though didn’t have many activities or events during the summer break, he focused on his passion for game development.9 He spent the time building a Roblox game using Lua programming for scripting and Roblox Studio for design.10 When Jerome entered his second year, his schedule became more intense, with classes from Monday to Thursday.11 Living in Rodriguez, Rizal meant long commutes, which made it hard for him to perform at his best due to fatigue.12 Nonetheless, he managed to finish the first semester with good grades and formed new friendships that made him feel loved and accepted.13 During the Christmas break, Jerome thoughtfully prepared gifts for the person he loves and spent the holidays joyfully with his family.14 As the new year began, he moved into a dormitory to make his school life easier and focus more on his studies.15 However, the second semester proved to be quite challenging.16 The heavy workloads and demanding major courses were overwhelming.17 Jerome was also preparing for the research topic he would need for his third year and capstone project.18 Despite the challenges, Jerome stayed focused on his goals, determined to keep moving forward, one step at a timeless.19 So far, this has been the life highlights of Jerome — from the day he was born to now, as he navigates college life.20
